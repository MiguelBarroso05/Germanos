Lisbon Tour

Explore the essence of Lisbon starting with São Jorge Castle, the Sé Cathedral, and the Church of Santo António. Wander through the winding streets of Alfama to the Casa dos Bicos, home of the Saramago Foundation. From there, pass by the iconic Praça do Comércio, crossing Chiado and Bairro Alto, before visiting the Parliament and the Casa de Fernando Pessoa. In Belém, indulge in the famous pastéis de Belém, visit the Coach Museum, the Jerónimos Monastery, the Belém Tower, and the Discoveries Monument. If you prefer, we can take you to the Oceanarium, the Parque das Nações, or museums of your choice, such as the Gulbenkian Museum, the Museum of Ancient Art, or the National Azulejo Museum.

We offer various programs in Lisbon, from 4-hour tours to full-day excursions, including stops at major tourist spots like Eduardo VII Park, Marquês de Pombal, Rossio, Sé Cathedral, and São Jorge Castle. For a more comprehensive experience, additional options include a ride on the iconic tram 28, visits to museums and studios, or an unforgettable fado night with dinner. If you prefer, opt for a 4-hour night tour of the city, including transportation and accompaniment to a fado restaurant.

Sintra Tour

Don’t miss exploring the Queluz National Palace and the charming town of Sintra, where you will find the famous Pena Palace, the Moorish Castle, Quinta da Regaleira, and the Royal Palace. A visit to Cabo da Roca, the westernmost point of mainland Europe, is also a must. Following the Atlantic coast, passing through Guincho and Cascais to Lisbon, you can enjoy the local cuisine with sea views, play golf on beautiful courses, or simply enjoy the beaches and Atlantic waves, whether for sunbathing or surfing.

The tours of the region include options of different durations and itineraries. The "Half-day Sintra" (5 hours) starts with a visit to Queluz and continues through Sintra, Cascais, and Estoril, returning to Lisbon via the Marginal Road. The "Sintra Circuit" (9 hours) is more comprehensive, including visits to Pena Palace and stops at various iconic locations such as Cabo da Roca and Guincho. The "Estoril Night" (5 hours) is a nighttime option with departure to Estoril and return via the motorway. For a longer tour, the "Mafra Circuit" (10 hours) includes a visit to Queluz Palace, Mafra Convent, with free time in Ericeira, and continues through Sintra and Guincho before ending in Lisbon.

Estoril Tour

Explore the charm of the Estoril Coast with a 5-hour tour that combines history, elegance, and stunning views of the Portuguese coastline. The tour begins with a meet at the hotel reception, followed by a departure along the famous Marginal Road, one of Portugal’s most scenic routes, which follows the Atlantic coast along the Tagus River. As we progress, you can enjoy panoramic views of the ocean and golden beaches.

The first stop is at the sophisticated town of Estoril, known for its glamorous past and the iconic Estoril Casino, the largest in Europe and inspiration for the famous James Bond novel "Casino Royale". Here, you will have time to explore the casino gardens, stroll along Tamariz Beach, or relax in one of the elegant seaside cafes. The tour may include a visit to the Estoril Palace, a symbol of luxury and history, famous for hosting European royalty during World War II. The excursion continues with a brief visit to the picturesque town of Cascais before returning to Lisbon along the same coastal road, enjoying the stunning late afternoon scenery. Return to the hotel at the end of the tour.

Fátima Tour

The 10-hour tour to Fátima offers a complete experience, starting with a visit to the Sanctuary of Fátima, where you can attend mass and explore the Basilica, the Chapel of Apparitions, and the new Church of the Holy Trinity. After lunch at the recommended "Tia Alice" restaurant, the tour continues to the Batalha Monastery, Alcobaça, Nazaré, Alfazeirão, and Óbidos, where you can taste the traditional ginjinha. The return to the hotel concludes the day, with the option to include a visit to the Convent of Christ and the Synagogue in Tomar.

The 10-hour itinerary to Tomar also includes a visit to Fátima but initially focuses on the city of Santarém and its historic attractions, such as the Church of S. Estêvão and the Church of Graça. After lunch in Almeirim, the tour proceeds to Almourol Castle and the town of Constância, before reaching Tomar to visit the Convent of Christ and optionally, the Synagogue. The day ends with a visit to the Sanctuary of F átima and the homes of the Little Shepherds, returning to the hotel. For a shorter version, there is a 5-hour option that focuses exclusively on the visit to the Sanctuary of Fátima with a direct return to Lisbon. A more extensive alternative includes Coimbra, with visits to the University and other historical points, in addition to Fátima, totaling 10 hours of touring.

Tour Tomar

Explore the rich history and culture of Tomar with a 10-hour tour that provides a comprehensive immersion into the region's traditions and historical landmarks. The journey begins with a pickup at the hotel reception, followed by a drive via the A1 to Santarém. There, you'll be guided through the Historic Center, visiting the Church of St. Stephen, renowned for the Miracle of the Sacred Host, and the Church of Grace, where the remains of the navigator Pedro Álvares Cabral rest. The visit includes a stop at the Portas do Sol, which offers a panoramic view over the Tagus River.

After lunch in Almeirim, where you can savor the traditional "sopa da pedra" (stone soup), the tour continues to the Castle of Almourol, situated on a small island in the Tagus River, and to Constância, the birthplace of Luís de Camões. In Tomar, the highlight is a visit to the imposing Convent of Christ, a gem of Templar and Manueline architecture, followed by an optional visit to the historic Tomar Synagogue, one of the oldest in Portugal. The tour can be customized with an additional visit to the Sanctuary of Fátima, where you can see the Basilica and the Chapel of the Apparitions. Return to the hotel at the end of the day.

Tour Arrábida

Discover the breathtaking landscapes and rich culture of the Arrábida region with an 8-hour tour that takes you to some of Portugal's most picturesque locations. The tour begins with a pickup at the hotel reception, followed by a crossing of the April 25th Bridge, offering stunning views of the Tagus River and Lisbon. The first stop is in the village of Azeitão, known for its vineyards and wine production. Here, you'll have the chance to visit a traditional winery, taste regional wines, and sample the famous Azeitão cheese tart.

The tour then continues through the Arrábida Natural Park, one of Portugal's natural treasures, where you can enjoy the unique landscape that combines mountains, forest, and the turquoise-blue Atlantic Ocean. We'll make a stop to admire the panoramic views from the highest points of the range and visit secluded beaches, such as Praia de Galápos or Praia do Portinho da Arrábida, known for their crystal-clear waters. The tour proceeds to the historic village of Sesimbra, where you can explore its medieval castle and picturesque fishing port. Before returning to Lisbon, there will be a stop at Cabo Espichel, where dramatic cliffs meet the ocean, providing an unforgettable view. Return to the hotel at the end of the day.

Tour Évora

Discover the charming city of Évora and the rich cultural heritage of the Alentejo with an 8-hour tour that provides an immersion into the historical treasures of this region. The tour begins with a pickup at the hotel reception, followed by a serene drive through the tranquil Alentejo landscape, known for its vast plains and vineyards. Upon arriving in Évora, a UNESCO World Heritage site, the first stop is at the impressive Praça do Giraldo, the heart of the city, where you can admire medieval architecture and the vibrant atmosphere.

The tour includes a visit to the Roman Temple of Évora, also known as the Temple of Diana, one of the best-preserved Roman monuments on the Iberian Peninsula. Next, explore the intriguing Chapel of Bones, located in the Church of São Francisco, one of Évora's most fascinating attractions, where walls and columns are decorated with human bones. The journey continues with a visit to the Évora Cathedral, one of Portugal's largest medieval cathedrals, offering panoramic views of the city. Before returning to Lisbon, there will be time for a traditional lunch at a local restaurant, where you can savor authentic Alentejo cuisine, known for its rich and unique flavors.

Tour Gastronómico

Option 1 - Cooking Class (9 Hours): Depart from Lisbon along the coast to Hotel Albatroz in Cascais, where participants will be welcomed by the Chef for a hands-on cooking class. The workshop includes a visit to the local market to purchase fresh ingredients for preparing a traditional Portuguese recipe. The program includes lunch and wine tasting. Afterwards, the tour continues to Sintra for a panoramic visit and a tasting of cheeses and wines in the medieval center before returning to the hotel.

Option 2 - Discover Sintra (9 Hours): Depart from Lisbon heading to Sintra, offering participants free time to explore Portugal's most romantic town. The journey continues to Colares for a wine tasting, followed by a relaxing lunch.

Option 3 - Moscatel Route (9 Hours): Depart from Lisbon towards Azeitão to visit the famous José Maria da Fonseca Winery. After a wine tasting, the tour includes a visit to an artisanal tile factory and a pass through the picturesque fishing village of Sesimbra. Return to Lisbon via the scenic Arrábida road, with a stop in Setúbal.

Option 4 - Cheese Workshop (9 Hours): Depart from Lisbon to Setúbal for a visit to Quinta do Viso, where participants are invited to learn how to make the region's traditional cheese. After the workshop, enjoy a family-style lunch followed by a drive along the scenic Arrábida road. The program includes a Moscatel tasting at the José Maria da Fonseca Winery and a visit to an artisanal tile factory before returning to Lisbon.
